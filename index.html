Labling:
Points can only be named as capital letters.

Lines can be labled by lowercase script(cursive), or 2 colinear points on the same line. Must always have a line, ray, or line segment on
top of letters.

Planes can be labled by putting "plane" in front of the points' names. Ex: Plane ABC

Segment addition postulate is a mathmatically unproven truth about adding segments onot other segments.

Midpoint:
If you are given two points and you want to find the mid-point, you can average the 2 numbers to get to the MIDpoint

Exaple: (2,4) and (1,9) are 2 points on a line and you need to find the midpoint. 2+1=3 then 3/2=1.5
So the first number is (1.5, ?). Do it again for the other point: 4+7=13 then 13/2=6.5



The midpoint is (1.5,6.5)

Angles:
The middle point is always in the middle between the 2 non colinear points. Ex: A connects to B and C but C and B don't connect to 
eachother so you can call the angle CAB or BAC but the A is always in the middle.
